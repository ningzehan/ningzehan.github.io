Analysis of Shenzhen Housing Price Data in Ten Years

Reported by: HAN Ningze   JIANG Xiaoqing   LI Zidan   HUANG Xuezhi

With your current monthly income,

how long would you need to buy a house in Shenzhen?

As a window of China's reform and opening up, Shenzhen is developing rapidly in all aspects. In the past five years, Shenzhen still maintains an annual increase of 400,000 to 500,000 permanent residents, and the population is still increasing continuously. As a city of immigrants, 63% of total residents in Shenzhen are non-native permanent residents. (21st Century Business Herald, 2020)

Lots of young migrants work in a senior office building downtown in the daytime and return to the city village at the night. They are dreaming of owning a flat of their own, but the high prices of housing beyond their scope of payment. 

LINEUP

Those people often poked fun at themselves that the market reshuffles them since they work hard in shenzhen in their prime but have to leave and come back to their hometown or other cities where's house pricing is afforadable for them since they can not afford to buy a house in shenzhen.

In recent years, Shenzhen's housing prices have been among the country's top three and often the highest. The average income of Shenzhen a decade ago was able to buy 3 square meters of space per year. By 2019, the average income of Shenzhen could only afford to buy 2.2 square meters per year.

Questions arose in our heads:

What happened in the last decade?  What elements possibly affect the house prices increasing?

As the second generation of the migrant in Shenzhen, we were intrigued to research Shenzhen's housing price. With the incentive to explore the fluctuation behind housing price as motivation, we proposed the following research questions:

The overall fluctuation of housing price

(All charts are interactive, please use the computer mouse to get a better experience)

We collected the overall average housing price data in Shenzhen from January 2011 to October 2020. From the line chart, we could find that the overall average housing price in Shenzhen shows an apparent increase from the year 2011 to 2020 with significant fluctuations from 2015 to 2017, climbing steadily to reach its peak in 2020. 

When further analyzing the curve, we can observe that there is a sharp rise in overall housing prices between March 2015 March 2016, with a year-on-year increase of 65%. We also notice that there is a sharp decrease from March 2016 to December 2016.

Policies that potentially effect the housing price

To explore the possible reasons behind this rapid rise, we viewed relevant websites, including Shenzhen Municipal Government Information Disclosure Platform and the Shenzhen Real Estate Information Platform, and we found some regulatory measures that might be related to it:

 

New policies were released on March 30, 2015:

a) Housing provident fund loans for purchasing the first self-owned housing dropped to a minimum down payment of 20%; b) families who own housing and have settled their loans could apply for housing provident fund again for house purchase, the minimum down payment is 30%; c) individuals who purchase ordinary houses for more than 2 years (including two years) for external sales are exempt from business tax.

The easing housing policies:

The tightening housing policies:

The municipal government has issued the "Opinions on Improving the Housing security System and Promoting the Steady and healthy development of the real estate market" on March 25, 2016, which contains the following main control measures: a) Increase housing supply through multiple channels; b) Improve the housing security system; c) Implement differentiated housing loan policies; d) Improve the house purchase regulation; e) Standardize the real estate market order; F) Strengthen the prevention and control of real estate financial risks.

Relations between housing price and income

There is no clear standard of the income ratio of house purchase in the academic field or in the market place, so we have made a standard ourselves. Here we assume that the size of a house is 50 square meters. So we multiply 50 times the average housing price per square meter, and this product is the "housing price" in the ratio.

The ratio of house price to income

The charts below show the ratio of housing price to income. The overall fluctuation of the ratio range from 15.8 to 25.69. 15.8 means that it takes 15.8 years without eating or drinking to afford a house of 50 square meters at the average price. When it comes to 2019, we can see that it was increased to 21.81, which devastatingly showed a person has to work more than 21 years at the same time saving all the money, to afford a house of 50 square meters.

Comparison of house price to income

Given that the ratio of house price to income is relatively rough, not rigorous data which can only roughly describe the relationship between the income and the house price in a city. So we directly make two broken lines to compare the average annual per capita income and the average house price (50 square meters), so that we can more intuitively see the gap between income and house price. Obviously, the gap between them is widening year by year.

According to our data, it will be a massive burden for young people living in Shenzhen to own a house. We often hear people around us and people on the internet saying that outstanding talents cannot be retained in Shenzhen. One of the most important reasons is that housing prices are too high. Especially in the traditional Chinese belief of one owning a house means genuinely belonging to the city. We expect that more policies will be carried out in the future to alleviate this phenomenon.
